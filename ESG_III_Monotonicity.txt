=====================================================================================
  PART 1: DEFINING ω_s FOR COMPLEX s
=====================================================================================


The problem: for real β, ω_β(n) = n^{-β}/ζ(β) is a probability.
For complex s, n^{-s} is complex. We need a REAL state.

APPROACH 1: Use |n^{-s}|² = n^{-2σ}.
  Then ω_σ(n) = n^{-2σ}/ζ(2σ), which depends only on Re(s) = σ.
  V(σ) = D_KL(ω_σ || ω_{1/2}) = D_KL(n^{-2σ}/ζ(2σ) || n^{-1}/ζ(1))
  This is monotonically increasing in |σ - 1/2| for σ > 1/2.
  But it contains NO information about γ = Im(s).
  VERDICT: True but useless for locating zeros.

APPROACH 2: The SPECTRAL MEASURE approach.
  Instead of deforming the STATE, deform the OBSERVABLE.
  For a fixed state ω_{1/2}, define the spectral function:
    F(s) = ω_{1/2}(Δ^{-s/2}) = Σ_n ω_{1/2}(n) n^{-s} ... 
  Wait, this is just ζ(s+1)/ζ(1), not quite.
  
  Actually: Tr(Δ^{-s/2}) = ζ(s) directly (Axiom E4).
  The zeros of ζ(s) are where the trace vanishes.
  
  The question should be reformulated WITHOUT deforming the state.

APPROACH 3: The RELATIVE PARTITION FUNCTION.
  Define Z(s, ω) = Σ_n ω(n) n^{-s} for any state ω on ℕ.
  For ω = ω_{1/2}: Z(s, ω_{1/2}) = Σ_n (n^{-1}/Z_1) n^{-s} 
                                    = ζ(s+1)/ζ(1) (regularized)
  
  The zeros of ζ(s) correspond to zeros of Z(s-1, ω_{1/2}).
  
  Define the FREE ENERGY:
    F(s) = -log|Z(s, ω_{1/2})| = -log|ζ(s+1)/ζ(1)|
  
  This is well-defined, real, and diverges at zeros of ζ(s+1).
  The zeros of ζ(s) at s = 1/2 + iγ become zeros of ζ(s+1) at
  s = -1/2 + iγ... that shifts things.

APPROACH 4: The DIRECT approach — no state deformation needed.
  The question "why do zeros lie on Re(s) = 1/2?" can be restated as:
  "Why does Σ n^{-s} = 0 only when Re(s) = 1/2?"
  
  For this, we don't need states at all. We need properties of the
  DIRICHLET SERIES Σ n^{-s} evaluated at the entropy-minimizing
  spectral measure (which is {1/n} normalized = ω_{1/2}).

  A(σ,γ) = Σ_n n^{-σ} cos(γ log n)
  B(σ,γ) = -Σ_n n^{-σ} sin(γ log n)

A zero requires A = B = 0 simultaneously.

The functional equation ζ(s) = χ(s) ζ(1-s) (where χ involves Γ)
gives symmetry under s ↔ 1-s on the critical strip.

For the COMPLETED function ξ(s) = ξ(1-s), with ξ real on the 
critical line: ξ(1/2 + iγ) ∈ ℝ.

when σ + iγ is reflected to 1-σ + iγ.

This means: |ξ(σ + iγ)|² = |ξ(1-σ + iγ)|².

So |ξ|² is SYMMETRIC around σ = 1/2. It achieves equal values

=====================================================================================
  PART 5: LOG-CONVEXITY OF |ζ|² IN σ
=====================================================================================


A stronger property than σ=1/2 being a minimum:
is log |ζ(σ+iγ)|² CONVEX in σ?

If log|ζ|² is convex in σ, then by the functional equation symmetry
(|ξ(σ)| = |ξ(1-σ)|), the minimum is at σ = 1/2 and the function
is monotonically increasing away from it.

Hadamard's three-circles theorem says: log M(r) is convex in log r
for analytic functions, where M(r) = max|f| on circle of radius r.

The Phragmén-Lindelöf principle gives similar convexity for strips.

For ζ specifically: log|ζ(σ+iγ)| is NOT generally convex in σ
in the critical strip (it has a pole at s=1 and zeros at s=1/2+iγ).

But the COMPLETED function ξ has no poles, so log|ξ(σ+iγ)| might
be convex in σ.

=====================================================================================
  PART 6: ENTROPY AS FUNCTION OF THE SPECTRAL PARAMETER
=====================================================================================


Instead of deforming the STATE, we can define an entropy that depends
on the spectral parameter s directly.

For the state ω_{1/2} (fixed), define:

  H(σ) = -Σ_n ω(n) log(n^{-σ} / Z(σ))
       = σ Σ_n ω(n) log(n) + log Z(σ)
       = σ ⟨log n⟩_ω + log ζ(σ)   (for σ > 1)

This is the CROSS-ENTROPY of ω_{1/2} with respect to the Gibbs
state at inverse temperature σ.

At σ = 1 (= β_c): H(1) = ⟨log n⟩_ω + log ζ(1) → ∞ (phase transition)

The RELATIVE cross-entropy (compared to σ = 1):
  ΔH(σ) = H(σ) - H(1) = (σ-1)⟨log n⟩_ω + log ζ(σ) - log ζ(1)

For complex s = σ + iγ:
  H(s) = s ⟨log n⟩_ω + log ζ(s)
  Re H(s) = σ ⟨log n⟩_ω + log|ζ(s)| + ...

The REAL PART of H(s) is related to log|ζ(s)|, which we tested above.

=====================================================================================
  PART 7: FISHER INFORMATION
=====================================================================================


The Fisher information of the family {ω_σ} at σ measures how
DISTINGUISHABLE nearby states are:

  I(σ) = Σ_n ω_σ(n) [d/dσ log ω_σ(n)]²
       = Σ_n ω_σ(n) [log(n) + ζ'(σ)/ζ(σ)]²

At σ = 1/2 (using ω_σ(n) = n^{-2σ}/ζ(2σ)):
  I(1/2) = Σ_n (n^{-1}/ζ(1)) [log(n) + ζ'(1)/ζ(1)]²

This measures the CURVATURE of the state manifold at σ = 1/2.
If I(σ) has a special property at σ = 1/2, it could be related
to the zeros.

The Fisher information is time-free (it's a property of the
statistical manifold, not of any dynamics).

=====================================================================================
  PART 8: WHAT THE COMPUTATION REVEALS
=====================================================================================


FINDINGS:

1. |ζ(σ+iγ)|² is NOT always minimized at σ = 1/2.
   For most γ values (away from zeros), the minimum is at σ = 1/2.
   But near zeros of ζ, the minimum can shift.
   MONOTONICITY CONJECTURE: partially true, not universal.

2. log|ζ(σ+iγ)|² IS approximately convex in σ for large γ,
   but NOT for small γ near the real axis.
   LOG-CONVEXITY: holds asymptotically, not uniformly.

3. The Fisher information I(σ) = 4 Var_{ω_σ}(log n) is maximized
   as σ → 1/2+ (from the right) and decreases for larger σ.
   This means: σ = 1/2 is where the state family {ω_σ} is 
   MOST SENSITIVE to parameter changes.
   FISHER INFORMATION: maximum at the critical point.

4. The entropy cost V(σ) = D_KL(ω_σ || ω_{1/2}) grows monotonically
   with |σ - 1/2| (because it depends only on σ, not γ).
   But this doesn't constrain the imaginary part γ.

HONEST ASSESSMENT:

The "entropy monotonicity" as originally conceived (V(s) grows away
from Re = 1/2) is TRUE for the real part but IRRELEVANT for the
imaginary part. The zeros of ζ involve BOTH real and imaginary
parts of s, and the imaginary part is where the constraint must come.

The correct characterization may be:
  σ = 1/2 is the unique point where:
  (a) ξ is REAL (functional equation)
  (b) The Fisher information is MAXIMAL
  (c) The entropy cost |V(σ) - V(1/2)| is MINIMAL
  
  Properties (a)-(c) together characterize the critical line,
  but proving that zeros can ONLY occur where all three hold
  simultaneously requires additional argument.

THE REMAINING GAP:
  We can characterize the critical line by three time-free properties.
  We cannot yet prove that zeros must occur ONLY on this line.
  
  The gap between "characterization" and "confinement" is where
  the difficulty of RH lives, regardless of the framework.
  
  ESG provides a TIME-FREE characterization. That is its contribution.
  The confinement remains the open problem of number theory.
