  PART 2: MITTAG-LEFFLER EXPANSION FROM KNOWN ZEROS
=====================================================================================


The Mittag-Leffler expansion of -d²/dβ² log ζ(β):

  ζ(s) has:
  - One pole at s = 1 (simple, residue 1)
  - Non-trivial zeros at ρ_k = 1/2 + iγ_k (and conjugates)
  - Trivial zeros at s = -2, -4, -6, ... (from Γ factor)

For the COMPLETED function ξ(s) = (s/2)(s-1)π^{-s/2}Γ(s/2)ζ(s):
  log ξ(s) = log(s/2) + log(s-1) - (s/2)log π + log Γ(s/2) + log ζ(s)

The Hadamard product gives:
  log ζ(s) = log ξ(s) - log(s/2) - log(s-1) + (s/2)log π - log Γ(s/2)
  
  d²/ds² log ζ(s) = d²/ds² log ξ(s) + corrections from Γ etc.

The Hadamard product of ξ:
  ξ(s) = ξ(0) Π_ρ (1 - s/ρ) e^{s/ρ}
  
  d²/ds² log ξ(s) = -Σ_ρ 1/(s-ρ)²

So: d²/ds² log ζ(s) = -Σ_ρ 1/(s-ρ)² + d²/ds²[correction terms]

The correction terms from the pole and Γ:
  -d²/ds² log(s-1) = 1/(s-1)²
  -d²/ds² log(s/2) = 1/s²  (but this is for ξ, small contribution)
  The Γ terms give the trivial zeros contribution.

For REAL β > 1:
  -d²/dβ² log ζ(β) = 1/(β-1)² + Σ_{ρ non-trivial} 1/(β-ρ)² + trivial corrections

=====================================================================================
  PART 8: HYPOTHETICAL OFF-CRITICAL ZERO
=====================================================================================


If a zero were at ρ = σ₀ + iγ₁ with σ₀ ≠ 1/2, how would C(β) change?

The contribution of a zero pair at σ₀ ± iγ is:
  2 Re[1/(β - σ₀ - iγ)²] = 2[(β-σ₀)² - γ²] / [(β-σ₀)² + γ²]²

For σ₀ = 1/2 (on critical line):
  2[(β-1/2)² - γ²] / [(β-1/2)² + γ²]²

For σ₀ ≠ 1/2 (off critical line):
  The symmetry ρ → 1-ρ means we must also include 1-σ₀ + iγ.
  Total: 1/(β-σ₀-iγ)² + 1/(β-σ₀+iγ)² + 1/(β-(1-σ₀)-iγ)² + 1/(β-(1-σ₀)+iγ)²
  = 2 Re[1/(β-σ₀-iγ)²] + 2 Re[1/(β-(1-σ₀)-iγ)²]
