  PART 5: RECONCILING WITH ESG I RESULTS

In ESG I (suppression test), we reported σ_KMS,min ~ φ(q).
Here we find σ_KMS,min ~ log(q)/q → 0.

The DISCREPANCY comes from different normalizations.

ESG I used:
  bkm_per_g = Σ_{(m,n):ratio≡g} 1/L(n/m)
  σ = 1 / (bkm_per_g / DIM)
  
  This gives σ = DIM / bkm_per_g = φ² / Σ 1/L

Here we use:
  avg_L = (1/φ) Σ_{(m,n):ratio≡g} L(n/m)
  σ = 1 / avg_L

The first (ESG I) computes the inverse of the HARMONIC MEAN of L.
The second (here) computes the inverse of the ARITHMETIC MEAN of L.

These are different! By AM-HM inequality:
  arithmetic mean ≥ harmonic mean

So avg_L ≥ φ/Σ(1/L), and therefore σ_here ≤ σ_ESG_I.

Let me check which one is the correct Jacobi eigenvalue.

RESOLUTION: ESG I had a normalization error.

Correct Jacobi eigenvalue: σ(g) = 1 / A_mean(L(g)) where
  A_mean(L(g)) = (1/φ) Σ_m L(n(m,g)/m)

This gives σ_min ~ log(q)/q → 0, NOT σ ~ φ(q) → ∞.

The ESG I result 'σ_KMS ~ φ(q)' was incorrect.
The correct result is: σ_KMS,min ~ log(q)/q → 0.

CONSEQUENCE: The 'growing rigidity' pillar of ESG falls.
